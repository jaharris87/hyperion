TESTING PHILOSOPHY

HYPERION uses two types of test: MAJOR and MINOR.

MAJOR tests correspond to releases, whereas MINOR tests correspond to
development.

The expectation for HYPERION testing is dependent on the release:

MAJOR AND MINOR RELEASES

- ALL MAJOR AND MINOR TESTS MUST PASS

Major releases should have adequate testing of new features. Removal of tests
can only occur if the removed test is superceded by a reasonable replacement.
Minor releases cannot remove existing tests for the corresponding major version
under any circumstances.

DEVELOPMENT

- ALL MAJOR TESTS MUST PASS

Any commit (on the master or primary development branches) must pass MAJOR
tests. MINOR tests can be implemented, deleted, and manipulated freely in
development. MINOR tests should be converted to MAJOR test before a major
release.
